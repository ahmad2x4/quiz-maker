## Question 7

**Topic:** Statistics • **Difficulty:** Medium • **Points:** 10

Calculate the mean from this frequency table:

| Score | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|
| 5     | 2         |
| 6     | 4         |
| 7     | 3         |
| 8     | 1         |

**Options:**
- A) 6.0
- B) 6.3
- C) 6.5
- D) 7.0

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>
**Correct Answer: B) 6.3**


### Solution

For a frequency table, use: $\bar{x} = \frac{\sum fx}{\sum f}$

Create an $fx$ column:

| Score | Frequency (f) | fx |
|-------|---------------|-----|
| 5     | 2             | 10  |
| 6     | 4             | 24  |
| 7     | 3             | 21  |
| 8     | 1             | 8   |
| **Total** | **10**    | **63** |

$$\bar{x} = \frac{63}{10} = 6.3$$

Therefore, the mean is **6.3** (Option B).

</details>

- B) 10

- D) 12

Therefore, the median is **10.5** (Option C).

A dataset has values: 12, 15, 18, 20, 22, 95

The mean is 30.33 and the median is 19. If the outlier (95) is removed, how does this affect the statistics?

**Options:**
- A) Both mean and median increase
- B) Both mean and median decrease
- C) Mean decreases significantly, median decreases slightly
- D) Mean increases, median stays the same

<summary>Show Explanation</summary>

Therefore, **Mean decreases significantly, median decreases slightly** (Option C).

## Question 24

**Topic:** Statistics • **Difficulty:** Hard • **Points:** 15

In a negatively skewed distribution, which relationship is typically true?

**Options:**
- A) Mean > Median > Mode
- B) Mode > Median > Mean
- C) Mean = Median = Mode
- D) Median > Mean > Mode

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>
**Correct Answer: B) Mode > Median > Mean**


### Solution

In skewed distributions:

**Negatively skewed** (tail left, data bunched right):
- **Mode** is highest (at the peak, on right)
- **Median** is middle
- **Mean** is lowest (pulled left by tail)
- Order: **Mode > Median > Mean**

**Positively skewed** (tail right, data bunched left):
- Order: Mean > Median > Mode

Therefore, **Mode > Median > Mean** (Option B) is correct for negatively skewed.

</details>

## Question 30

**Topic:** Statistics • **Difficulty:** Hard • **Points:** 15

A dataset has: mean = 50, median = 48, mode = 45. Based on this, what can you conclude about the distribution?

**Options:**
- A) The distribution is symmetrical
- B) The distribution is negatively skewed
- C) The distribution is positively skewed
- D) The distribution is uniform

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>
**Correct Answer: C) The distribution is positively skewed**


### Solution

Analyze the relationship between mean, median, and mode:

Given: Mode (45) < Median (48) < Mean (50)

**Positively skewed**: Mean > Median > Mode (data bunched left, tail right)
**Negatively skewed**: Mode > Median > Mean (data bunched right, tail left)
**Symmetrical**: Mean ≈ Median ≈ Mode

Since Mode < Median < Mean, this indicates the distribution is **positively skewed** with a long tail to the right pulling the mean higher.

Therefore, **The distribution is positively skewed** (Option C) is correct.

</details>
