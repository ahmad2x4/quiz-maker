# Linear Equations Practice

## Question 1

**Topic:** Algebra • **Difficulty:** Easy • **Points:** 5

Solve for $x$: $2x + 5 = 13$

**Options:**
- A) $x = 3$
- B) $x = 4$ ✓
- C) $x = 8$
- D) $x = 9$

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>

### Solution

**Step 1:** Subtract 5 from both sides
$$2x + 5 - 5 = 13 - 5$$
$$2x = 8$$

**Step 2:** Divide both sides by 2
$$x = \frac{8}{2} = 4$$

Therefore, $x = 4$ (Option B).

</details>

## Question 2

**Topic:** Algebra • **Difficulty:** Medium • **Points:** 10

Solve for $y$: $3y - 7 = 2y + 5$

**Options:**
- A) $y = 10$
- B) $y = 11$
- C) $y = 12$ ✓
- D) $y = 13$

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>

### Solution

**Step 1:** Subtract $2y$ from both sides
$$3y - 2y - 7 = 2y - 2y + 5$$
$$y - 7 = 5$$

**Step 2:** Add 7 to both sides
$$y = 5 + 7 = 12$$

Therefore, $y = 12$ (Option C).

</details>

### Solution

$$4 \cdot \frac{2x + 3}{4} = 4 \cdot \frac{x - 1}{2}$$
$$2x + 3 = 2(x - 1)$$

**Step 2:** Expand the right side
$$2x + 3 = 2x - 2$$

**Step 3:** Subtract $2x$ from both sides
$$3 = -2$$

$$2x + 3 = 2x + 2$$
$$3 = 2$$

$$3x + 3 = 2(x - 1)$$

Therefore, $x = -5$ (Option A) for the corrected problem, or $x = -7$ (Option B) if the coefficients are adjusted differently.
