A square has a perimeter of 24 cm. What is the length of one side?

**Options:**
- A) 4 cm
- B) 6 cm
- C) 8 cm
- D) 12 cm

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>
**Correct Answer: B) 6 cm**


### Solution

A square has 4 equal sides.

**Perimeter of square** = 4 × side length

$$24 = 4 \times \text{side}$$

Divide both sides by 4:
$$\text{side} = \frac{24}{4} = 6 \text{ cm}$$

Therefore, the length of one side is **6 cm** (Option B).

## Question 10

**Topic:** Geometry • **Difficulty:** Medium • **Points:** 10

A sector of a circle has a radius of 8 cm and a central angle of 90°. What is the area of the sector?

**Options:**
- A) 16π cm²
- B) 32π cm²
- C) 16π cm²
- D) 64π cm²

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>
**Correct Answer: C) 16π cm²**


### Solution

The area of a sector is:
$$A_{\text{sector}} = \frac{\theta}{360°} \times \pi r^2$$

Where θ = 90° and r = 8 cm:

$$A_{\text{sector}} = \frac{90°}{360°} \times \pi \times 8^2$$

$$= \frac{1}{4} \times \pi \times 64$$

$$= 16\pi \text{ cm}^2$$

Therefore, the area is **16π cm²** (Option C).

**Decimal approximation:** 16π ≈ 50.27 cm²

</details>

## Question 21

A cylinder has a volume of 1000π cm³ and a height of 10 cm. What is the radius of the cylinder?

**Options:**
- A) 5 cm
- B) 10 cm
- C) 15 cm
- D) 20 cm

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>
**Correct Answer: B) 10 cm**


### Solution

The volume formula for a cylinder is:
$$V = \pi r^2 h$$

We know V = 1000π cm³ and h = 10 cm. Substitute:
$$1000\pi = \pi r^2 \times 10$$

Divide both sides by π:
$$1000 = r^2 \times 10$$

Divide both sides by 10:
$$r^2 = 100$$

Take the square root:
$$r = 10 \text{ cm}$$

Therefore, the radius is **10 cm** (Option B).

## Question 24

**Topic:** Geometry • **Difficulty:** Hard • **Points:** 15

The International Space Station completes one circular orbit of Earth at a height of 400 km. If the radius of Earth is approximately 6400 km, find the distance travelled by the station in one orbit. (Give your answer correct to the nearest km)

**Options:**
- A) 40,212 km
- B) 42,726 km
- C) 45,239 km
- D) 47,753 km

<details>
<summary>Show Explanation</summary>
**Correct Answer: B) 42,726 km**


### Solution

The ISS orbits at a height of 400 km above Earth's surface, so the orbital radius is:
$$r_{\text{orbit}} = r_{\text{Earth}} + \text{height} = 6400 + 400 = 6800 \text{ km}$$

The distance travelled in one orbit equals the circumference of the circular path:
$$C = 2\pi r = 2 \times \pi \times 6800$$

$$= 13{,}600\pi$$

$$\approx 42{,}726 \text{ km}$$

Therefore, the ISS travels approximately **42,726 km** (Option B) in one orbit.

</details>
